============================================================
KEY FINDINGS SUMMARY
============================================================

1. UNDERSTANDING GAP (SIGNIFICANT): Students rate their understanding of
   professors' LLM policies (M=3.85) higher than professors' understanding
   of student use (M=3.02), p < 0.01. Perception of asymmetric knowledge.

2. ETHICS-BEHAVIOR GAP: 93% believe LLMs can be used ethically per Catholic
   mission, yet 49% admit school misuse. Clear divide between principle & practice.

3. SCHOOL vs PERSONAL: 49% school misuse vs 25% personal — stronger norms
   around personal life than academics.

4. FORCE FOR GOOD: Modestly skeptical (M≈2.59). No relationship with CST or
   faith importance (Bonferroni corrected). SIGNIFICANT: aligns with Univ values (r=0.56, p=0.0000).

5. BY GRADE: No significant difference (Kruskal-Wallis p=0.75). Graduate
   students largest (n=20). Sophomores highest school misuse (59%).

6. BY COLLEGE: Engineering (n=21) most positive on force for good (M=3.07),
   52% school misuse. Arts & Letters (n=12) most skeptical (M=1.83), 42% misuse.

7. DAILY vs WEEKLY USERS: Daily users 61% school misuse vs 38% weekly/less
   (ns, p=0.146). More use associates with more admitted misuse.

8. LOW CONCERN = MORE MISUSE: Those with low academic integrity concern
   (1-2) report 67% school misuse vs 46% for high concern (4-5).

9. BY GENDER: No significant differences. Men slightly more positive on
   force for good (M=2.86 vs 2.43), 48% vs 50% school misuse (ns).
