KEY FINDINGS SUMMARY
============================================================

1. UNDERSTANDING GAP (SIGNIFICANT): Students rate their understanding of
   professors' LLM policies (M=3.94) higher than professors' understanding
   of student use (M=3.04), p < 0.01. Perception of asymmetric knowledge.

2. ETHICS-BEHAVIOR GAP: 90% believe LLMs can be used ethically per Catholic
   mission, yet 45% admit school misuse. Clear divide between principle & practice.

3. SCHOOL vs PERSONAL: 45% school misuse vs 22% personal — stronger norms
   around personal life than academics.

4. FORCE FOR GOOD: Modestly skeptical (M≈2.53). No relationship with CST or
   faith importance (Bonferroni corrected). Trend: aligns with Univ values (r=0.00, p=1.0000).

5. BY GRADE: No significant difference (Kruskal-Wallis p=0.99). Graduate
   students largest (n=26). Sophomores highest school misuse (59%).

6. BY COLLEGE: Engineering (n=25) most positive on force for good (M=2.98),
   52% school misuse. Arts & Letters (n=16) most skeptical (M=1.84), 38% misuse.

7. DAILY vs WEEKLY USERS: Daily users 59% school misuse vs 32% weekly/less
   (ns, p=0.200). More use associates with more admitted misuse.

8. LOW CONCERN = MORE MISUSE: Those with low academic integrity concern
   (1-2) report 67% school misuse vs 40% for high concern (4-5).

9. BY GENDER: No significant differences. Men slightly more positive on
   force for good (M=2.76 vs 2.38), 48% vs 43% school misuse (ns).
